# One-Person Content Research Team

**Source:** Brad Bonanno — "I Built a One-Person Content Research Team Using AI - Claude Cowork Feels ILLEGAL" (January 2026, 17 min)

## What it is

Brad's Claude Cowork setup as a content research operation: research, fact-checking, topic ideation, competitive analysis — run by a single person with AI doing the team's work.

His description: "feels ILLEGAL" — implying the productivity gain is so large it shouldn't be possible.

## What the "team" does

- Researches topics across multiple sources simultaneously
- Identifies trending content opportunities
- Pulls competitive intelligence
- Synthesizes findings into actionable briefs
- Drafts content outlines

All within Claude Code, with Claude acting as every team member simultaneously.

## The Business Case

A content research team typically costs:
- Researcher: $50K–$80K/year
- Editor: $60K–$100K/year
- Content strategist: $70K–$120K/year

Total: $180K–$300K/year for a small team.

The AI system doing this work: Claude Code subscription + time to set up.

## For Selling

Target: content businesses, agencies, publishers, marketing teams. The pitch is headcount replacement — not "AI helps your team" but "AI is your team."
