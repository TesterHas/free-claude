# AI-Native Processes

**Source:** Brad Bonanno — "AI-Native Explained In 5 Minutes" (January 2026)

## The One-Question Test

> "Who is the process designed around?"

- **AI-assisted:** Process designed for humans, AI tools added to help. ~1.2x efficiency gain.
- **AI-native:** Process designed entirely around AI capabilities, humans step in where needed. ~10x efficiency gain.

Same quality of outcome. Radically different starting point.

## The Real Example

Brad's SaaS requirements gathering case:

| Approach | Process | Time | Efficiency |
|----------|---------|------|------------|
| AI-assisted | Meeting bot takes notes, fills spreadsheet during human-led sessions | 10 hrs → ~8 hrs | 1.2x |
| AI-native | Customer works directly with AI agent to build requirements; agent fills gaps and flags issues; consultant attends one focused wrap-up session | 1–3 hrs | 10x |

The AI-assisted approach added AI to the bottleneck. The AI-native approach removed the bottleneck.

## The Trap

Most businesses start with "where can we add AI?" — that's the wrong question. It leads to AI-assisted at best. The right question: "If we rebuilt this entirely around what AI does well, what would the process look like?"

## The Pitch

When a prospect says "we use AI tools already" — ask them which processes are AI-native vs AI-assisted. Usually the answer is 0 AI-native. That's your gap to fill.

An AI-native redesign of even one process can produce 10x efficiency. One proof-of-concept wins the relationship.

## Where This Fits

This is the **positioning** frame for selling AI systems — not "here's a tool that makes your current process faster" but "here's a redesigned process that's 10x more efficient." The former is a feature. The latter is a transformation.
